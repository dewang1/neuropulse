INSTRUCTIONS

Step 1

Strap on the Arduino shell and battery pack to forearm and adjust the straps for tightness

Step 2

Peel plastic off three new electrodes and snap them to the muscle sensor. Also, attach new electrode pad to the purple wire.

Step 3

Place the Myoware sensor electrode pads at the indicated locations.

Step 4

Plug in the battery pack wire into the Arduino Nano to turn the device on!

Step 5

Sign up and log in to your account. Then, click Start Data Collection on the home page. Your sensor is now recording your nerve's conductivity!

Step 6

Continue using the sensor every day. You can view your past data either by choosing a date or month.

FAQ


It's designed for ease of use and can be set up quickly within minutes. It is lightweight, comfortable, and can be worn during everyday activities. Our device comes with adjustable straps to ensure a comfortable and secure fit for everyone.


Many users have reported significant benefits from using our device, including early detection of CTS, which has allowed them to take proactive measures such as ergonomic adjustments and seeking medical attention sooner. This early intervention has helped prevent further nerve damage, reduce pain, and improve overall hand and wrist function. Additionally, users appreciate the peace of mind that comes from monitoring their nerve health daily.


If the device detects potential CTS symptoms, we recommend making ergonomic adjustments, reducing repetitive wrist activities, and seeking medical advice for further evaluation and management.


For optimal results, we recommend wearing the device daily, especially during activities that involve repetitive wrist movements.


While our device provides early detection of CTS symptoms, it is not a replacement for a professional medical diagnosis. If you experience symptoms, we recommend consulting with a healthcare provider.


The device starts monitoring immediately upon use. However, it may take weeks of data to have a clear understanding of your situtuation.

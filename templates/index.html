NeuroPulse: Detect and Prevent Carpal Tunnel Early

Preventative wearables for a healthier you!

Product Overview

Our user-friendly wearable device helps combat carpal tunnel syndrome (CTS) through early detection. An electrode on the forearm outputs electrical signals, which are measured by a Myoware surface electromyography (sEMG) sensor on the hand to sense if CTS is developing.

Why?

CTS is a condition caused by compression of the median nerve in the wrist, often resulting from overuse in activities such as typing and writing, leading to pain, numbness, and tingling in the hand and arm.

Traditional methods often identify CTS only after symptoms manifest, when irreversible damage to the median nerve may have already occurred.

Our device allows early detection and intervention, which prevents further damage and enables users to adopt ergonomic adjustments and lifestyle modifications, or seek medical attention.

How?

Our device allows for real-time measurement of median nerve conductivity throughout daily activities. Conductivity is measured with an electrode on the forearm that outputs electrical signals, which are measured by the sEMG sensor on the hand.

Our website displays the data collected in an intuitive interface, which can be shared with a medical professional. Individuals can then use this information to take proactive measures to mitigate the condition's progression.

Data

The real-time data from the MyoWare sensor is transmitted to our online platform. The website features an intuitive interface that presents detailed trends and analysis based on the collected data.

Users and their medical professionals can track changes over time, identify potential risk factors, and receive timely alerts or recommendations for preventive measures.
